Patrice Lumumba

First democratically elected prime minister of the DRC

A timeline of Patrice Lumumba's life

  • Born July 2, 1925.
  • October 1959. Jailed by Belgian colonial regime for organizing protests demanding decolonisation and the independence of Congo.
  • January 1960. Released from prison after much international pressure and participated in the international conference in which Belgium was forced to cede independence.
  • May 1960. The Mouvement national congolais, co-founded by Lumumba, won Congo’s first national election. Patrice Lumumba became Congo’s founding prime minister.
  • September 1960. Lumumba’s government was overthrow by Congolese military officers working in league with Belgium and the United States. He was placed under house arrest, guarded by UN soldiers. He later escaped from house arrest.
  • Dec. 1, 1960. Arrested and jailed in very harsh conditions.
  • January 17, 1961. Murdered while in detention. The actual murder was carried out by Belgian-backed secessionists of Katanga province commanded by a Belgian officer at a Belgian-controlled military site. The record of the CIA’s collaboration with Belgium in deposing and then murdering Patrice Lumumba has been proven by the documentary record, including commissions of inquiry by the Belgian and the U.S. governments.
